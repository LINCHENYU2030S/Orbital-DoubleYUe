Strategy 1
Mean-reversion Strategy

Mean-reversion is a trading strategy that operates on the premise that asset prices tend to revert to their historical average over time. The Simple Moving Average (SMA) is a key component of this strategy, representing the average price of the asset over a specific period, such as 30 days. The SMA period defines the time frame used to calculate the average. The z-score threshold determines the level of deviation from the SMA at which the strategy triggers a trade signal. If the z-score exceeds the threshold, indicating the asset is overbought or oversold, it generates a signal to either sell or buy, aiming to capitalize on potential price reversals back towards the historical mean.

Strategy 2
Moving Average Cross Over Strategy
This strategy involves using two moving averages of different periods, such as a short-term moving average (e.g., 50-day SMA) and a long-term moving average (e.g., 200-day SMA). When the short-term moving average crosses above the long-term moving average, it generates a "Buy" signal, and when the short-term moving average crosses below the long-term moving average, it generates a "Sell" signal.

Strategy 3
Bollinger Bands Strategy
Bollinger Bands consist of a middle line (usually a simple moving average) and two standard deviation lines above and below the middle line. The upper and lower bands act as dynamic resistance and support levels, respectively. When the price crosses above the upper band, it generates a "Sell" signal, and when the price crosses below the lower band, it generates a "Buy" signal.
