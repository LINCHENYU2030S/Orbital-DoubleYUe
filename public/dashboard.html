    Part 1 / 8
    What is Trading?

    Trading, in simple terms, is the act of buying and selling financial instruments (like shares, forex and indices), in the hopes of making a profit from changes in their price movements.
    Part 2 / 8
    What is a Trading Simulator?
    A trading simulator is computer software that reproduces behavior and features of a stock market, so that a user may practice trading stocks without financial risk.
    Part 3 / 8
    Why should we need a Trading Simulator?
    A trading simulator allows you to hone your skills and develop strategies about trading without risking actual money.
    Part 4 / 8
    Leverage
    Leverage in trading enables you to open a position worth much more than the money you deposit. (For example, a 1: 100 leverage means for every $1 in your balance, you can trade up to $100)
    Part 5 / 8
    Margin
    A small deposit that can be used to control a large amount of an asset. We call this deposit: standard margin or initial margin. This is the absolute minimum you need in your account to open and maintain a position.
    Part 6 / 8
    Market Volatility
    Market volatility is the frequency and magnitude of price movements, up or down. The bigger and more frequent the price swings, the more volatile the market is said to be.
